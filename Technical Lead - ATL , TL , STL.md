## ⭐ REO Leadership Framework for Associate Technical Leads

### The REO Practice (Levels 1–3)

REO (Reflect, Connect, Reorient) is the foundational leadership framework at all technical leadership levels. As an Associate Technical Lead, you directly manage developers and are accountable for conducting effective REO sessions. This framework is cascaded:

**Associate Technical Lead**
- **Manages:** Software Developers
- **REO Frequency:** Conducted once every two months
- **Focus:** Technical development, sprint delivery, removing individual blockers

**Technical Lead**
- **Manages:** Associate Technical Lead
- **REO Frequency:** Conducted once every two months
- **Focus:** Operational leadership skills, workflow optimization

**Senior Technical Lead**
- **Manages:** Technical Lead
- **REO Frequency:** Conducted once every two months
- **Focus:** Strategic technical leadership, business alignment

---

### Implementing REO for Associate Technical Leads

**R — Reflect**
- **What to do:** Listen deeply, acknowledge feelings, understand perspectives
- **Sample questions:** "What's energizing you in your work?" "Where do you feel stuck?"
- **Associate Technical Lead focus:** Understanding developer challenges with technical tasks, blockers, collaboration

**C — Connect**
- **What to do:** Bridge individual contribution to team goals, create shared understanding
- **Sample prompts:** "Your API work connects to the dashboard project..." "Here's how this fits to our sprint goals..."
- **Associate Technical Lead focus:** Connecting technical work to overall project goals and business impact

**R — Reorient**
- **What to do:** Provide specific, actionable feedback for growth
- **Use SBI model:** Situation-Behavior-Impact
- **Associate Technical Lead examples:** "In yesterday's standup (situation), you mentioned a blocker but didn't escalate it (behavior). This risks sprint delivery (impact). Let's agree you'll escalate blockers within 4 hours going forward..."

### Associate Technical Lead REO Best Practices

- **Documentation:** Keep brief notes of each REO session in a consistent location, focused on action items and growth opportunities
- **Preparation:** Prepare a 3-bullet agenda for each session to ensure focused discussion
- **Follow-up:** Track action items and refer back to previous discussion points to build trust
- **Frequency:** Conducted REO session once every two months is the minimum standard; adjust based on developer needs
- **Ownership:** Associate Technical Lead owns scheduling REO sessions with developers — don't wait to be asked

## 🔁 Transition Lens (From Developer to Tech Lead)

**The Junior Technical Lead – Associate Technical Lead framework marks a fundamental shift in accountability:**

### ➡️ From "My Code" to "Our Output"
Your success is now measured by *the team's delivery*, not your personal pull requests. You've transitioned from a consumer of work flow to its orchestrator.

### ➡️ From "Solving Problems" to "Unblocking Solvers"
Your technical skills are now primarily applied to help others service their development needs — not to deliver features yourself. You increase team velocity by clearing their unique paths.

### ➡️ From "Isolated Contributor" to "Feedback Catalyst"
You now own the *REO rhythm* with developers as a vital component of your role — ensuring consistent feedback, growth, and connection between individual work and team goals.

### ➡️ From "Reporting Status" to "Curating Truth"
You are the single source of truth for status — requiring intense accuracy, proactive updates, and holistic perspective for all stakeholders.

### ➡️ From "Managing My Time" to "Stewarding Team Time"
You now protect your team's focus, ensure accurate tracking, and verify that recorded time properly reflects the team's true effort distribution.

---

### ⭐ Your Success Is Measured By...

The elimination of communication gaps between requesters and developers, the consistency of the team's delivery rhythm, the clarity of the shared knowledge base, **the quality and regularity of REO sessions with developers**, and the quality of insights you provide to stakeholders about forward-looking risks. This role is the critical linchpin for team performance. You eliminate chaos, create clarity, and build the operational rhythm that allows developers to focus on what they do best: building.

---

> ⚠️ **Critical Performance Insight**
>
> Junior Technical Leads must dedicate **at least 70% of their time** to operational leadership activities (task assignment, progress monitoring, communication flows). Any less risks reintroducing the disconnect between strategic vision and execution that your role is designed to solve. **REO sessions with developers are a non-negotiable component of your responsibilities.**
>
> **REO Implementation Note:** Each Associate Technical Lead should conduct REO sessions with developers using the three-component framework:
> - **Reflect:** Deep listening to understand developer contributions, challenges, and perspectives
> - **Connect:** Making clear how their work fits into the team's overall goals and objectives
> - **Reorient:** Providing specific, actionable feedback to guide continuous improvement
>
> This consistent leadership rhythm builds psychological safety, clarifies expectations, and drives growth without relying solely on people's ability to "be curious."
>
> **Remember:** If an L1 Lead is consistently spending >30% of their time on code, they are likely hindering team operational efficiency in the belief they are helping. **Associate Technical Leads are accountable for conducting effective REO sessions with developers they manage.**

### ⭐ Leadership Philosophy

"As a Senior Technical Lead, success isn't demonstrated in the work you *do*, but in the quality systems you *build and govern*. Your leadership culture should consist of regular, structured interactions focused on these three pillars that increase in frequency as leadership accountability rises:

- **Reflect:** Active listening to understand performance and challenges
- **Connect:** Meaningful conversation to build alignment and shared understanding
- **Reorient:** Providing developmental direction through structured feedback

These components form the REO framework — which for Technical Lead & Senior Technical Lead — is the consistent leadership rhythm that builds authentic relationships through purposeful dialogue and structured growth."

> ⚠️ **Critical Performance Insight**
>
> Senior Technical Leads must dedicate **at least 50% of their time** to strategic activities (technical roadmap, syndication of practices across business units, leadership development). Your impact is measured by organizational self-sufficiency, trust from business leadership, and **reliable implementation of REO and leadership frameworks across Technical Lead & Senior Technical Lead**. Remember that REO is explicitly designed as a Technical Lead & Senior Technical Lead practice where Immediate Reporting Persons (IRPs) must ensure all supervisor interactions include the essential components: **Reflect** (active listening), **Connect** (meaningful conversation), and **Reorient** (constructive feedback for improvement).
>
> **REO Culture as Strategic Imperative**
>
> At Senior Technical Lead, you don't just participate in REO sessions — you're responsible for building a culture where they're the consistent rhythm of leadership. Every interaction between Technical Lead & Senior Technical Lead should naturally incorporate these three components to:
> - Build psychological safety through active listening (Reflect)
> - Create strategic alignment with business goals (Connect)
> - Drive continuous improvement through structured feedback (Reorient)
>
> This leadership framework ensures that engagement happens through purposeful dialogue, not just periodic check-ins. It builds team confidence and intellectual debt management while maintaining visible leadership expectations. Use your quarterly talent reviews to assess not just individual performance but the health of this leadership culture across the organization.
